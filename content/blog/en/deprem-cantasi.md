Earthquakes are an unavoidable reality in many regions. In the first hours after a disaster, before professional emergency response teams can reach you, the most crucial preparation to survive and meet your basic needs is preparing an **Emergency and Earthquake Kit**.

In this guide, you will find what should be in your kit for your home, office, or vehicle, and the critical rules to keep in mind.

---

## 🎒 What Should Be in an Earthquake Kit?

Grouping items into 4 main categories will help you organize your bag more effectively:

### 1. Food and Water (Supply for at least 72 hours)
*   **Water:** At least 1.5 - 2 liters of drinking water per person per day.
*   **Food:** High-calorie, nutrient-dense, non-perishable, and canned foods (bars, nuts, canned tuna, etc.).
*   **Can Opener:** Essential to open the food cans in your bag.

### 2. First Aid and Health Supplies
*   **First Aid Kit:** Bandages, sterile gauze, band-aids, antiseptic solution, scissors, and medical tape.
*   **Personal Medications:** Spares of your regular prescription medications.
*   **Hygiene Items:** Soap, wet wipes, toothbrush, toothpaste, sanitary pads, and garbage bags.

### 3. Equipment and Essential Tools
*   **Whistle:** The most critical tool to make yourself heard if trapped under rubble or in an emergency.
*   **Flashlight:** Battery-powered flashlight and plenty of spare batteries.
*   **Radio:** Battery-powered or hand-crank radio to receive information from the outside world.
*   **Multi-tool Pocket Knife:** For cutting, opening, and general utility.
*   **Mylar Thermal Blanket:** To maintain body temperature and protect from cold weather.

### 4. Documents and Cash
*   Photocopies of important documents like ID cards, driver's licenses, deeds, insurance policies, and passports (in a waterproof bag).
*   Some cash (since ATMs and credit card terminals won't work if the power grid goes down).

---

## ⚠️ Golden Rules to Keep in Mind

1.  **Check Expiration Dates:** Review and refresh the food, water, and batteries in your kit at least once every 6 months.
2.  **Keep it Accessible:** Store your earthquake bag near the exit door, along your evacuation route, in a cabinet everyone in the household knows.
3.  **Watch the Weight:** The kit must be light enough for you to quickly grab and carry on your back in an emergency (ideally, it should not exceed 10-15% of your body weight).

*Remember, preparation reduces fear and saves lives.*
